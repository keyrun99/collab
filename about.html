About This Project

3D Utility & Infrastructure Planning at Ontario Parks

The main focus of our project was creating a detailed 3D model of an area within Emily Provincial Park, incorporating both underground utility infrastructure and above-ground facilities. The goal of this model was to give the park's maintenance and planning staff a framework and powerful decision-making tools so that they could obtain accurate data about the location, size, and depth of subterranean utilities. Through the use of the LMX200 Ground Penetrating Radar for data gathering, we were able to effectively overcome a number of obstacles that arose throughout the project's implementation.

Beyond enhancing safety measures within Emily Park, our efforts aimed to safeguard the well-being of park visitors by creating a comprehensive risk analysis model. This included a multi-criteria risk assessment, tree proximity risk analysis, and flood simulation risk analysis. By integrating these findings, the maintenance department can now modify our framework to make informed decisions on how to effectively mitigate these potential hazards.

This website briefly outlines the methodology, challenges faced, solutions implemented, and the outcomes achieved throughout the project. It also underscores the significance of our findings in advancing maintenance strategies and safety protocols for Emily Provincial Park.

Prior efforts included preliminary mapping and traditional utility documentation, which highlighted the challenges posed by outdated data and inconsistencies in underground utility records.

The client representative for this project was Josh Richer, a GIS Database Technician for Ontario Parks at MECP, who played a crucial role in guiding the project's objectives and ensuring alignment with the park's management and maintenance needs.

Our study area - Emily Provincial Park (Figure 1) - is located in Omemee, covering an area of around 87.56 hectares beside Pigeon River. The park has over 300 campsite facilities and is managed by Ontario Parks, a part of the Ministry of the Environment, Conservation, and Parks in Ontario, Canada.
